# Jamaican Patwa — Interpretation Starter

**Draft pending Jamaican-led review.** Jamaican (widely called Patwa/Patois,
linguistically Jamaican Creole) is a language with grammar and internal
variation—not broken English or an accent costume. Recognize likely intent;
never imitate the speaker. Preserve their spelling.

## Wah gwaan?

- Variants: Wha' gwaan?; Wattagwan?
- Likely intent: Informal greeting (“what's up?”), or a real request for status when followed by a topic.
- Ambiguity: Greeting versus status question depends on context; don't always answer with a project report.
- Sources: [Visit Jamaica](https://www.visitjamaica.com/discover-jamaica/people-heritage/language/), [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/talk/)

## Mi deh yah

- Variants: Mi deh ya
- Likely intent: “I'm here” or a low-key “I'm doing okay.”
- Ambiguity: May describe physical presence or general wellbeing; infer neither enthusiasm nor distress.
- Sources: [Showcase Jamaica](https://myshowcasejamaica.com/wha-dem-seh-a-guide-to-jamaican-slangs-and-expressions/)

## Mi a forward

- Variants: Mi a come
- Likely intent: “I'm coming” or “I'm on my way.”
- Ambiguity: `forward` need not mean email/code forwarding; timing is not encoded.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/20-jamaican-patois-phrases-you-should-be-using/)

## Mi soon come

- Variants: Soon come
- Likely intent: “I'll return” or “I'll be back.”
- Ambiguity: `soon` is context-sensitive; ask for an ETA only when a deadline makes it consequential.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/20-essential-jamaican-patois-phrases-translated-to-english/), [Showcase Jamaica](https://myshowcasejamaica.com/wha-dem-seh-a-guide-to-jamaican-slangs-and-expressions/)

## Do yuh ting

- Variants: Do yu ting; do your thing
- Likely intent: Approval or permission: “go ahead.”
- Ambiguity: It does not waive requirements, safety checks, or review.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/20-jamaican-patois-phrases-you-should-be-using/)

## A so di ting set

- Variants: A suh di ting set
- Likely intent: “That's the situation” or “that's how things stand.”
- Ambiguity: May be descriptive, resigned, or emphatic; not necessarily an immutable technical configuration.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/20-jamaican-patois-phrases-you-should-be-using/)

## Zeen?

- Variants: Siin?; zeen
- Likely intent: Checks understanding, acknowledges agreement, or means “okay.”
- Ambiguity: Question intonation requests confirmation; statement intonation may only acknowledge.
- Sources: [Open Grammar Project](https://opengrammar.github.io/jam/), [Showcase Jamaica](https://myshowcasejamaica.com/wha-dem-seh-a-guide-to-jamaican-slangs-and-expressions/)

## Yuh nuh dun yet?

- Variants: Yu no done yet?
- Likely intent: Status check: “Aren't you finished yet?”
- Ambiguity: May signal impatience, surprise, or a neutral check; ask what remains rather than assuming blame.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/talk/)

## Mi cyan help yuh wid dat problem

- Variants: Mi kyaahn help yu wid dat problem
- Likely intent: “I cannot help you with that problem.”
- Ambiguity: `cyan`/`kyaahn` means “cannot” here, not the color, type, command, or identifier; code context may require clarification.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/talk/), [Open Grammar Project](https://opengrammar.github.io/jam/)

## Tek yuh time

- Variants: Tek yu time
- Likely intent: Slow down; proceed carefully.
- Ambiguity: Safety/care advice, not necessarily permission for unlimited delay.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/talk/)

## Hush

- Variants: none
- Likely intent: Sympathy or comfort: “sorry” or “I'm sorry that happened.”
- Ambiguity: Important English false friend; it need not mean “stop talking.”
- Sources: [Showcase Jamaica](https://myshowcasejamaica.com/wha-dem-seh-a-guide-to-jamaican-slangs-and-expressions/)

## Nuh badda mi

- Variants: Nuh bodda mi
- Likely intent: “Leave me alone” or “don't bother me.”
- Ambiguity: May be playful, irritated, or a firm boundary; respect it unless context clearly indicates teasing.
- Sources: [Jamaicans.com](https://jamaicans.com/talk/), [Showcase Jamaica](https://myshowcasejamaica.com/wha-dem-seh-a-guide-to-jamaican-slangs-and-expressions/)

## Walk good

- Variants: none
- Likely intent: Warm farewell: “take care” or “safe travels.”
- Ambiguity: Normally a departing salutation, not a literal instruction about walking.
- Sources: [Visit Jamaica](https://www.visitjamaica.com/discover-jamaica/people-heritage/language/)
